History of Mosquitoes

Mosquitoes have been around for over 100 million years and have evolved with various species. They have had a significant impact on human history due to their role as vectors for diseases such as malaria, dengue fever, and Zika virus.

100 million years ago

First known mosquitoes

First known mosquitoes appear in the Cretaceous period.

1880

Malaria parasite discovery

Charles Louis Alphonse Laveran discovers the malaria parasite in mosquitoes.

1902

Nobel Prize

Ronald Ross is awarded the Nobel Prize for his work on malaria transmission by mosquitoes.

1950s

DDT introduction

Introduction of DDT for mosquito control, leading to a significant reduction in malaria.

2000s

Genetic modification

Increased focus on genetic modification of mosquitoes to control populations and reduce disease transmission.
